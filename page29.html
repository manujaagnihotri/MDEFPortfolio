Digital Prototyping

Digital prototyping is the process of creating a virtual representation of a product using computer-aided design (CAD) software before physically building the actual prototype. It allows industrial design students to visualize, test, and iterate their designs in a digital environment, saving time and resources compared to traditional prototyping methods.

4. Testing and Simulation

Digital prototyping is a valuable tool that enhances the design process, encouraging creativity, efficiency, and informed decision-making in industrial design.

Learning about how to create biomaterials was a very important lecture and allowed me to realize how dependent I am on just plastic usage. To create a biomaterial that could be laser cut into a lamp was the aim for the project to allow myself to learn how to create biomaterial and also be able to fabricate it. I first made a few test batches to see which would be the most rigid of all and could withstand the heat of a laser cutting machine. After having spoken with Petra, my decision was to make a biomaterial from either Agar Agar or Gelatin due to both their rigidity properties. I added a little fiber to both for extra rigid properties and upon drying, it was final that I had to create my larger batch of biomaterial using gelatin.
